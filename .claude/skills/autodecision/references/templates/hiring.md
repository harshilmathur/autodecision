# Template: Hiring Decision

Use when deciding whether and how to hire — new roles, team expansion,
leadership hires, contractor vs full-time, or organizational restructuring.

## Pre-populated Sub-Questions

1. **Need validation:** Is this a real capacity gap, or can existing team absorb the work differently?
2. **Role definition:** What exactly does success look like in 6 months? Is the role well-defined?
3. **Market and timing:** Can we attract the right person at our stage, compensation, and timeline?
4. **Team dynamics:** How does this hire change the team's dynamics, communication patterns, and culture?
5. **Alternative paths:** Could we solve this with contractors, automation, process change, or deprioritization instead?

## Pre-populated Constraints

- Must have someone productive within [user specifies] months
- Compensation budget: [user specifies] range
- Cannot compromise on [user specifies] culture/quality bar

## Suggested Search Queries (Phase 1: GROUND)

- "{role} salary benchmark {market} {year}"
- "{role} hiring timeline average {company stage}"
- "early stage startup {role} vs contractor tradeoffs"
- "bad hire cost startup impact"
- "{role} job market supply demand {year}"

## Domain-Specific Personas Enhancement

- **Growth Optimist:** Focus on leverage this hire creates, unblocked initiatives, speed gains
- **Risk Pessimist:** Focus on bad-hire cost (typically 1.5-2x salary), culture dilution, management overhead
- **Competitor Strategist:** Focus on talent market competition, poaching risk, compensation arms race
- **Regulator:** Focus on employment law, equity/compensation compliance, misclassification risk (contractor vs FTE)
- **Customer Advocate:** Focus on how this hire improves user experience, support quality, product velocity

## Key Assumptions to Watch

- Time to productivity (usually 3-6 months, not "immediately")
- Availability of the right candidate at your compensation level
- Whether the role is well-defined enough to hire for (vague roles = bad hires)
- Manager capacity to onboard effectively
