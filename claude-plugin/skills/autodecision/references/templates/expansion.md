# Template: Market Expansion

Use when the decision involves entering a new market — geographic, vertical,
segment, or channel expansion.

## Pre-populated Sub-Questions

1. **Market demand:** Is there real demand in the target market, or are we projecting our assumptions?
2. **Localization/adaptation:** What changes to product, pricing, support, and go-to-market are needed?
3. **Competitive landscape:** Who already serves this market? How entrenched are they?
4. **Execution complexity:** What's the operational cost — hiring, partnerships, compliance, infrastructure?
5. **Cannibalization:** Does the new market compete with or complement existing business?

## Pre-populated Constraints

- Must show positive signal (pipeline, LOIs, early revenue) within [user specifies] months
- Budget cap of [user specifies] for initial market entry
- Cannot degrade service quality in existing markets

## Suggested Search Queries (Phase 1: GROUND)

- "{target market} {product category} market size {year}"
- "{target market} startup expansion case study success failure"
- "{target market} regulatory requirements {industry}"
- "cost of market entry {target market} {industry}"
- "{competitor} {target market} strategy"

## Domain-Specific Personas Enhancement

- **Growth Optimist:** Focus on TAM, first-mover advantage, network effects in new market
- **Risk Pessimist:** Focus on burn rate, distraction from core, execution complexity
- **Competitor Strategist:** Focus on incumbent defense, local competition, partnership landscape
- **Regulator:** Focus on local regulations, data residency, employment law, tax implications
- **Customer Advocate:** Focus on product-market fit, localization quality, support availability

## Key Assumptions to Watch

- Product-market fit transferability (HIGH sensitivity — the #1 expansion killer)
- Local competitive response speed and intensity
- Hiring and operational cost estimates (usually underestimated 2x)
- Regulatory complexity (usually underestimated)
