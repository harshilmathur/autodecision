# Template: Pricing Change

Use when the decision involves changing prices — increases, decreases, restructuring,
or introducing new tiers.

## Pre-populated Sub-Questions

1. **Customer acquisition impact:** How will the price change affect new customer acquisition rate and CAC?
2. **Existing customer response:** How will current customers react — churn, satisfaction, expansion, renegotiation?
3. **Competitive response:** How will competitors respond — match, differentiate, ignore, exploit?
4. **Unit economics:** What happens to ARPU, CAC payback, LTV, and margin at the new price point?
5. **Reversibility:** Is this change reversible? What's the cost of reverting if it fails?

## Pre-populated Constraints

- Must maintain positive unit economics within [user specifies] months
- Cannot lose more than [user specifies]% of existing customers
- Must not trigger contractual MFN clause renegotiations (if applicable)

## Suggested Search Queries (Phase 1: GROUND)

- "{industry} pricing change customer acquisition impact case study"
- "SaaS price {increase/decrease} churn rate data"
- "{competitor name} pricing strategy {year}"
- "B2B pricing change unit economics benchmark"
- "price elasticity of demand {industry}"

## Domain-Specific Personas Enhancement

- **Growth Optimist:** Focus on acquisition volume, market share capture, viral/referral effects
- **Risk Pessimist:** Focus on churn, revenue cannibalization, irreversible price anchoring
- **Competitor Strategist:** Focus on price matching, differentiation, narrative weaponization
- **Regulator:** Focus on MFN clauses, revenue recognition (ASC 606), contractual obligations
- **Customer Advocate:** Focus on perceived fairness, price-value alignment, switching costs

## Key Assumptions to Watch

- Price sensitivity of the target market (HIGH sensitivity — test empirically)
- Volume offset viability (joint probability trap — require conjunctive analysis)
- Competitor monitoring and response speed
- Reversibility of price anchoring effects
