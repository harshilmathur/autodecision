## Executive Summary

- **Decision:** Should Adobe deprecate Photoshop's UI-first model within 3 years and go all-in on agentic Creative Cloud?
- **Recommendation:** **No, do not deprecate.** Instead, ship a segmented dual-SKU structure: "Photoshop Pro" (UI perpetual + 10-year LTS commitment) alongside "Photoshop Agentic" (Firefly AI Assistant native), with binding public reversal triggers. This captures agentic growth without irreversible franchise risk.
- **Confidence:** MEDIUM-HIGH on "do not aggressively deprecate" (HIGH); MEDIUM on the segmented+LTS structure being executable.
- **Hypotheses explored:** 6 — aggressive transition wins (conditional), user revolt destroys franchise (risk path), hybrid additive dominates (Adobe's actual April 2026 strategy [G15]), competitive window closes (conditional on foundation labs), segmentation correct (conditional on segment separability), and the promoted h6 segmented+LTS+reversal-triggers structure.
- **Deepest disagreement:** Competitive urgency (h4) vs franchise preservation (h2, h3). Optimist scored 55% [C2:optimist] probability that first-mover agentic lock compounds; Pessimist scored 75% [C2:pessimist] probability of reversal-impossible franchise damage. The disagreement IS the data: urgency claim depends on foundation labs entering creative by 2028 (probability 0.5 [C3:council]).
- **Dominant risk:** Irreversibility after announcement (P=0.75 [C4:pessimist]) combined with partial agentic awkward state at 3-year mark (P=0.55 [C5:competitor]) — 30-year Photoshop feature surface cannot ship agentic parity in 36 months. Adversary identified 8 of 12 scenarios push toward KILL or SEGMENTED.
- **Load-bearing assumption:** Adobe can ship agentic parity with 30 years of Photoshop workflow primitives within 36 months [C6:regulator]. Sensitivity analysis shows this is HIGH-risk — if parity takes 60-72 months (likely), the 3-year deprecation guarantees the partial-agentic awkward state.

## Recommendation

**Action:** **Do NOT aggressively deprecate the Photoshop UI within 3 years.** Instead, execute the segmented dual-SKU structure: (a) ship "Photoshop Pro" with UI perpetual + binding 10-year LTS commitment, (b) ship "Photoshop Agentic" as Firefly AI Assistant native new SKU, (c) publish binding reversal triggers (if pro churn exceeds 8% [C17:council] OR agentic ARPU falls below threshold, revert or extend LTS), (d) quarterly user council of 50 top pros reviews progress. Note that Adobe's announced April 2026 Firefly AI Assistant [G15] is already the additive (h3) subset of this recommendation; the structural improvement is to formalize the dual-SKU + LTS + reversal triggers to capture both cohorts and convert the implicit additive strategy into an explicit segmented one.

**Confidence:** HIGH on "do NOT aggressively deprecate" (the aggressive path survives <15% [C3:council] of plausible scenarios). MEDIUM-HIGH on the segmented+LTS structure being the optimal alternative. MEDIUM on Adobe board willingness to publish binding reversal triggers (requires legal + shareholder comms alignment).

**Confidence reasoning:** Two iterations of 5-persona council converged on h6 with 5/5 endorsement. Adversary's dominant finding (irreversibility = structural vulnerability) directly validates the binding reversal trigger mechanism. Sensitivity analysis identifies 3 HIGH-sensitivity assumptions (agentic paradigm terminal, 36-month parity, foundation lab entry) — if any ONE fails, aggressive deprecation is indefensible. Adobe's own April 2026 Firefly AI Assistant launch [G15] is the strongest real-time data point: Adobe chose additive, not deprecation. The leading alternative is a modest structural upgrade (add segmented SKU + LTS + reversal triggers) to the strategy Adobe is already executing.

**Depends on:**
  - Adobe board legal willing to publish binding public reversal triggers [C17:council]
  - LTS commitment on Pro SKU is credible enough for pro community trust
  - Segments (pros vs prosumers) are cleanly separable with dual-SKU pricing
  - Adobe execution on shared Firefly backend amortizes dual-SKU R&D cost
  - Foundation labs' agentic creative entry timeline allows 6-12 month preparation window
  - AI Act + copyright indemnity compliance infrastructure holds through 2027

**Monitor:** (a) Firefly AI Assistant beta adoption and NPS from pro segment (weeks post-launch); (b) Acrobat UI backlash resolution [G3] — test case for Adobe's willingness to listen; (c) OpenAI/Anthropic creative product announcements (any native creative vertical launch = accelerate h6 implementation); (d) Figma capital deployment post-IPO (M&A targeting Runway/Midjourney = competitive signal); (e) EU AI Act enforcement cases against Firefly training-data claims; (f) Enterprise ELA renewal cohort Q2-Q4 FY26 — watch for procurement pushback on UI continuity clauses; (g) ML talent retention — senior ML engineer attrition above 10% [C1:council] annualized is structural red flag.

**Pre-mortem:** Top 5 failure modes. (1) Adobe attempts h6 without binding reversal triggers — collapses into h1 with all attendant franchise risk (P=0.3 without explicit commitment). (2) Foundation model plateau by 2027 renders Photoshop Agentic SKU under-featured, forcing either retrenchment or pushing transition timeline out (P=0.25 [C23:adversary]). (3) Pro community rejects LTS as marketing-only despite contractual commitment; churn happens anyway (P=0.2). (4) Internal resourcing split between Pro and Agentic SKUs produces both at below-quality vs single-focus competitor (P=0.55 [C39:council]). (5) Elliott/Starboard-style activist pressures board to "just commit to agentic" reversing segmented strategy mid-execution (P=0.15 [C34:adversary]).

**Review trigger:** Re-evaluate within 6 months if: (a) Firefly AI Assistant beta NPS below 40 among pros; (b) OpenAI or Anthropic ships native creative product; (c) Adobe board refuses to publish binding reversal triggers; (d) Acrobat UI backlash resolution turns hostile (proxy for Adobe's willingness to listen); (e) Q2 FY26 Digital Media segment growth drops below 10% [C1:council]; (f) Federal government (GSA) flags Section 508 concerns with Firefly AI Assistant.
