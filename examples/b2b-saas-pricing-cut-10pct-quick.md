## Hypotheses

1. **10% cut drives enough volume to offset revenue loss** — Unlikely in enterprise (inelastic), plausible only in SMB segment
2. **10% cut triggers competitor matching, net zero benefit** — High probability (60%+ match rate), erodes market margins
3. **10% cut is too small to matter** — Lands in the "dead zone" for enterprise procurement; not large enough to change vendor selection, large enough to compress margins
4. **Restructure pricing model instead** — Hybrid/usage-based models showing 38% higher revenue growth; addresses the same growth goal without a permanent cut

## Recommendation

- **Action:** Do not implement a blanket 10% price cut. The cut is too small to meaningfully move enterprise conversion (inelastic segment), too large to ignore in margin impact (3-7 points of gross margin compression), and highly likely to be matched by competitors (60%+ probability), erasing any temporary advantage. Instead, consider: (1) a segmented SMB-only promotional price or starter tier, (2) a usage-based or hybrid pricing component that gives customers a lower entry point without permanently cutting headline price, or (3) a time-limited 10% discount for annual commitments to test elasticity before committing to a permanent cut.
