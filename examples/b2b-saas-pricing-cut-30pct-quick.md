## Hypotheses

1. **30% cut drives 43%+ volume increase** — Extremely unlikely. B2B sales cycles are 3-9 months; achieving 43% pipeline expansion in <12 months while maintaining quality is near-impossible.
2. **30% cut wins deals but destroys margins** — Pyrrhic victory. CAC payback extends to 18-24 months, LTV drops 30%+, fundraising multiples compress.
3. **30% cut signals desperation** — High probability. Cutting 30% when the market is raising 8-25% annually reads as distress to enterprise buyers and investors.
4. **30% cut works IF paired with model restructure** — The only scenario where a large reduction makes strategic sense: it's a new entry tier in a land-and-expand motion, not a cut to the existing price.
5. **Restructure to hybrid/usage-based instead** — Captures same price-sensitive demand without permanent anchor reset. Already proven at 38% revenue growth premium.

## Recommendation

- **Action:** Do not implement a blanket 30% price cut. The math is brutally unfavorable: you need 43% more volume to break even, LTV research shows discounting at this level drops lifetime value by 30%+ (compounding, not offsetting), and 2026 is a market where everyone else is raising prices 8-25%. A 30% cut in this environment reads as desperation to enterprise buyers, triggers existing customer renegotiation demands (80% probability), and permanently resets your price anchor (80% probability) — making future price increases 3-5x harder. Instead: (1) if the goal is to win price-sensitive SMB deals, create a lower-cost entry tier or usage-based plan that doesn't touch your enterprise pricing, (2) if the goal is to compete on price, run a 90-day competitive win-back campaign with time-limited discounts that don't reset the anchor, (3) if the goal is growth acceleration, invest the margin you would have burned (10-20 points of gross margin) into sales headcount, product-led growth, or channel partnerships instead.
