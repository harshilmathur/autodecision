# Decision Brief: University — make in-person attendance optional given AI tutoring?

**Mode:** Full | **Iterations:** 2 | **Convergence:** REACHED | **Council:** 5 personas | **Generated:** 2026-04-18

---

## Executive Summary

- **Decision:** Should a mid-to-top-tier research university (R1/R2, 15-40K students) make in-person attendance optional given that AI tutoring now outperforms lecture on learning outcomes?
- **Recommendation:** **Do NOT make attendance universally optional. Instead, execute the phased-structured path (h6): 3-year opt-in pilot in 1-2 departments + mandatory first-year residential + dual-track F-1/Title IV compliance structuring (required in-person seminar components per semester) + faculty senate/union as co-design partners + formal AI-hybrid teaching model mirroring the Harvard 2025 RCT protocol.**
- **Confidence:** HIGH that pure universal optional attendance is strictly dominated (regulatory catastrophe + equity failure + faculty revolt + donor damage). MEDIUM-HIGH on the phased-structured alternative being executable.
- **Hypotheses explored:** 6 — outcomes improve (conditional, self-direction-dependent), social product degrades (real for elite tier), faculty revolt (if unilateral), enrollment bifurcation (mid-tier vulnerable), de-facto already optional (scope-limited), phased-structured with safeguards (NEW, promoted).
- **Deepest disagreement:** Optimist scored 60% [C1:optimist] probability of learning outcome gains from AI-hybrid vs Pessimist's 25% [C1:pessimist]. Disagreement driven by confidence in replicating Harvard RCT at institution scale vs skepticism on self-direction heterogeneity [C2:customer].
- **Dominant risk:** Regulatory catastrophe. F-1 SEVP violation (P=0.85 [C3:regulator]) puts $30-150M international revenue at risk; Title IV RSI rule reclassification (P=0.65 [C4:regulator]) risks federal aid eligibility of $50-300M/yr. Without structured compliance workarounds, the decision is self-enforcing collapse.
- **Load-bearing assumption:** Peer network IS the real product of the university (not content). If true, optional attendance erodes tuition justification and donor relations. Council range 0.4-0.8 [C5:council] on this assumption — binary on whether h2 social damage is material.

**Persona legend:** Optimist (Growth + Creative Strategist), Pessimist (Risk Pessimist), Competitor (Competitor Strategist), Regulator (Constraint Analyst), Customer (Customer Advocate).

- **Strongest: Regulator (#1 peer rank).** Won on specific regulatory mechanisms that other personas missed — F-1 SEVP violation (P=0.85 [C3:regulator]), Title IV RSI reclassification, FERPA DPA with AI vendors, accreditor substantive change trigger. Each of these is a potential existential threat to the decision.
- **Weakest: Optimist (#5 peer rank).** Overweighted Harvard RCT results (N=194) as representative of institution-scale deployment; under-weighted self-direction heterogeneity that Customer persona caught.
- **Key disagreement: Harvard RCT replicability at institution scale.** Optimist scored 60% [C1:optimist] probability; Pessimist + Customer scored 25% [C1:pessimist]. If RCT replicates, h1 outcomes dominate; if not, only structured AI-hybrid works.
- **Uncertainty hotspot: F-1/Title IV structured compliance.** Council range 0.55-0.75 [C13:iter2council] on whether required in-person seminar components can be structured to satisfy SEVP + RSI. Binary regulatory gate for h6.
- **Consensus surprise: 4 of 5 personas converged on phased-structured variant.** Pessimist's pilot-one-department + Customer's mandatory-first-year + Regulator's dual-track compliance + Competitor's elite-weaponize-but-pilot-hedges + Optimist's AI-hybrid coaches all point to h6.

## Stable Insights

1. **Pure universal optional attendance is self-enforcing collapse.** Title IV RSI + F-1 SEVP + faculty union + donor relations + equity impact all converge on catastrophic outcome. The decision as stated (universal) is dominated.
2. **AI tutoring DOES outperform lecture on knowledge transfer.** Harvard 2025 RCT [G1] is robust, replicated in multiple smaller studies. Effect size 0.7-1.3 SD.
3. **Human-AI hybrid > AI-only > classroom-only** [G3]. The pedagogical winning structure includes humans.
4. **Peer network IS real product for elite tier.** Council range 0.4-0.8 but top-end consensus that elite tuition premium is network-justified.
5. **Adversarial scenarios converge on structural solutions.** 8/12 scenarios defused by phased-structured approach; 4 remain (foundation model breakthrough, Ivy preempts, campus event, admin change).
6. **Minerva + WGU + online MBAs prove partial-optional works at extremes.** Elite alt (Minerva) and cost alt (WGU) succeed; middle tier most vulnerable to pure pivot.

## Fragile Insights

1. **Harvard RCT replicates at institution scale.** Holds at Harvard; uncertain at 30K-student R1. **Boundary:** If outcomes lag in first-gen + commuter cohorts, h1 conditional status is proven.
2. **Faculty senate/union partners with administration.** Holds if governance process invites them early. **Boundary:** Unilateral policy without bargaining triggers h3 revolt path.
3. **Required in-person seminars satisfy F-1/Title IV.** Legal interpretation — depends on DOE + SEVP rulings. **Boundary:** If interpreted strictly, structured workaround fails.
4. **Pilot data is credible after 3 years.** Depends on assessment methodology + selection bias controls. **Boundary:** If pilot too small (<200 students) or self-selected high-achievers, results won't generalize.
5. **Mid-tier bifurcation really happens.** Historical precedent from online MBA market. **Boundary:** If employers treat ALL schools equivalently regardless of attendance, bifurcation collapses.

## Recommendation

**Action:** Do NOT make attendance universally optional. Execute the phased-structured path: (a) launch 3-year opt-in pilot in 1-2 departments (e.g., Computer Science, Philosophy — high AI-tutor fit AND non-clinical); (b) mandatory first-year residential preserved across ALL programs; (c) dual-track accreditation filing + required in-person seminar components per semester to satisfy F-1 SEVP and Title IV RSI; (d) faculty senate + union formally brought in as design partners BEFORE policy changes; (e) adopt Harvard RCT-style AI-hybrid teaching model (AI tutoring + human discussion seminars); (f) publish outcomes + enrollment + faculty + equity data annually with external advisory board review.

**Confidence:** HIGH that pure universal optional attendance is strictly dominated. MEDIUM-HIGH on the phased-structured alternative being executable (depends on regulatory structuring viability + faculty cooperation).

**Confidence reasoning:** Two iterations of 5-persona council converged on h6 with 5/5 endorsement. Adversary's dominant finding: phased-structured defuses 8/12 existential-risk scenarios (joint probability drops from 0.75-0.85 unstructured to 0.20-0.30 structured). Sensitivity analysis identifies 3 HIGH-sensitivity assumptions (F-1 SEVP enforcement, Title IV RSI, peer network product). The Harvard RCT [G1] provides the pedagogical foundation.

**Depends on:**
  - F-1 SEVP one-online-course rule enforced against new model [C3:regulator]
  - Title IV RSI rule reclassifies optional + AI as correspondence [C4:regulator]
  - Required in-person seminar components satisfy SEVP and RSI [C13:iter2council]
  - Union contracts prohibit unilateral attendance change [C9:council]
  - AI-hybrid teaching model replicates Harvard RCT at institution scale [C1:optimist]
  - First-year cohort formation is disproportionately important [C14:iter2council]
  - Peer network IS the real product of university [C5:council]

**Monitor:** (a) DOE guidance on RSI and correspondence classification; (b) SEVP guidance on in-person seminar components; (c) Faculty union contract renegotiation calendar; (d) Pilot cohort outcome metrics (knowledge transfer, critical thinking, retention) year 1-3; (e) First-gen retention rate vs control departments; (f) Peer university announcements (Harvard/Stanford/MIT staying in-person, state systems moving toward AI-hybrid); (g) Donor sentiment surveys quarterly; (h) ChatGPT-6 / foundation model capability benchmarks.

**Pre-mortem:** Top 5 failure modes. (1) Faculty union rejects partnership and strikes mid-semester (P=0.15 [C35:council]); pre-bargain; invite union into AI-hybrid teaching design from day 1. (2) Pilot data inconclusive after 3 years due to selection bias (P=0.35 [C40:council]); design with RCT rigor; random assignment within consented cohorts. (3) F-1 SEVP structured compliance fails legal review (P=0.3 [C41:regulator]); pre-file with SEVP; legal safe harbor via in-person seminar requirement. (4) First-gen dropout spike despite mandatory first-year (P=0.2 [C42:council]); deep scaffolding in first year + opt-in residential advising. (5) Mid-tier positioning fails (elite preempts AND state undercuts) (P=0.3 [C43:council]); position as "AI-hybrid research-teaching lab" not "cost-cutting optional attendance."

**Review trigger:** Re-evaluate within 18 months if: (a) DOE issues negative guidance on RSI classification; (b) SEVP clarifies one-online-course rule strictly; (c) Peer institution (Harvard/Stanford/MIT) announces similar pilot; (d) Pilot cohort year-1 outcomes show negative equity impact; (e) Faculty union strikes or files formal grievance; (f) ChatGPT-6 or equivalent makes current AI-hybrid design obsolete; (g) Major donor announces giving cut >$5M [C1:council] tied to this policy.
